The second wave of COVID-19 crisis put extreme and lasting strain on our medical infrastructure

At Swasth, we worked with our Alliance members and our team of volunteers to bring people the resources they need to battle the pandemic.

We aimed to create systems where patients can care for themselves with ease at home, and thereby reduce load on hospitals and clinics for patient care. We additionally worked diligently to provide patients with oxygen concentrators from major cities to remote parts of India.

| Oxygen for Everyone |

About half of the population infected with COVID-19 required oxygen support

Swasth believes that easy access to oxygenation at patient’s nearby medical facilities is critical to better health outcomes in many diseases including COVID-19. Swasth started working on strengthening the Oxygen capacity in India in October 2020. This work was substantially scaled up through the Oxygen For Everyone program during the second wave of COVID-19 in India between April to July 2020. We continue to work on providing oxygen equipment across the country and support in multiple ways to strengthen the health system.

Oxygen Concentrator Pilot

October 2020 to March 2021

With the ACT Grants support, deployed 160 Oxygen Concentrators in multiple districts across India. These concentrators were deployed with the help of various local governments and trusted local NGO partners including the likes of SEARCH (Gadchiroli), Basic Health Services (Dungarpar), Jan Swasthya Sahyog (Bilaspur) among others.

Oxygen For
Everyone

April and July 2021

As the second wave of COVID-19 pandemic spread across India, we realized access to Oxygen equipment will be crucial to save lives. Along with our partners ACT Grants and Feeding India, we started the Oxygen For Everyone program in which we deployed more than 50,000 devices.

Ongoing

Principles of allocation we followed during Wave 2:

EQUITY

We used data-driven decision making to identify areas and facilities most in need to allocate the limited supply of oxygen concentrators. While the media focused on the crisis in major urban centres, the principle of equity dictated that we also work on supporting rural districts that have far less infrastructure, resources and visibility in the mainstream and social media. To support the principle of inclusion of the poor and marginalized sections of society, our distribution was prioritizing public health facilities and charitable hospitals.

TRANSPARENCY

We are committed to transparency. All details of OCs distributed are published in the public domain on the MyGov, ACT and Swasth Alliance website. The methodology is published on MyGov (mygov.in), Swasth (swasth.app) and ACT websites, along with district-level distribution of oxygen concentrators.

IMPACT

We deployed OCs in the most impacted regions, as rapidly as possible. To identify these regions, we developed a methodology which Leveraged epidemiological data to allocate concentrators across districts of India. This methodology took into account publicly available COVID-19 data, and was further refined using predictive analytics generated by the Aarogya Setu ITIHAS interface (Developed by IIT Madras) for emerging hotspots.

How we work

Supply and Procurement

We are working with a large base of suppliers from across the world whom we evaluate through a vendor due diligence and specifications alignment. We lock in supply by making bulk purchases (for lower prices and reduced volatility).

Demand Generation

With the generous donation of the Federation of Indian Associations (FIA) US, ACT Grants, Swasth Alliance, Social Alpha, and United Way Bangalore will be donating BiPAP machines to healthcare facilities all over India.
